About this tool

This tool provides you access to the model that our research team has created. The model calculates a risk value for nearly every location in the state of Iowa. The value is calculated relative to other locations that are known to have palmer infestations. The model takes into account factors outside of a farmers control such as average highest temperature, soil quality, elevation, rainfall and wind as well as demographic information to compute a risk value. The final risk value represents how similar or dissimilar the location is to places known to be infested with palmer.

The table below provides more details about what factors lead to a high or low risk calculation. The results of our model are not an indication that Palmer has been sighted, but a high risk value does mean that the conditions are right for Palmer Amaranth to grow in your area.
